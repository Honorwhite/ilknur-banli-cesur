Advantages of Newborn Circumcision and Things to Consider

Newborn circumcision is a surgical procedure performed within the first 28 days (preferably the first week) after the birth of the baby. In addition to its many medical benefits, it is frequently preferred by parents due to the short recovery process.

Why Newborn Circumcision?

The biggest advantage of circumcision performed in the newborn period is that the babies' pain threshold is high in this period and tissue healing occurs very quickly. Also, it can be performed safely with local anesthesia without requiring general anesthesia.

Newborn circumcision is one of the most effective protection methods in preventing the risk of urinary tract infection and circumcised skin inflammations (balanitis) that may occur in later ages.

Process and Recovery

  • Local Anesthesia: The procedure can be completed calmly while the baby is being breastfed or sleeping.
  • Rapid Recovery: Full recovery is usually achieved within 3-5 days.
  • Psychological Comfort: The child does not remember the circumcision process, which eliminates medical anxieties.
  • Low Risk of Bleeding: The coagulation mechanisms of newborns are compatible with this process.

Following your doctor's recommendations regarding the baby's diaper change and bath processes after the procedure is sufficient for a smooth recovery.
