Pediatric General Surgery

Pediatric General Surgery Specialties

Pediatric general surgery is a comprehensive department that deals with the surgical diseases of the organs located in the chest and abdominal cavity (esophagus, stomach, intestines, liver, spleen, etc.) and soft tissues in children. Assoc. Prof. İlknur Banlı Cesur, one of the reference names in this field in Adana, offers scientific and compassionate solutions to children's surgical problems.

Many of the diseases within the scope of general surgery are situations that require emergency intervention (such as appendicitis) or increase the quality of life (such as hernias). Our goal in these operations is to ensure that the child regains their health with minimum surgical trauma, without disturbing their physiological balance. With the surgical services we provide in a modern hospital environment, our children continue to smile with confidence.

Common General Surgery Operations

The most common general surgery cases in childhood are as follows:

  • Inguinal Hernia
  • Umbilical Hernia
  • Acute Appendicitis (Laparoscopic/Open)
  • Constipation and Anal Fissure
  • Gastric Reflux and Gastrostomy applications
  • Neck Cysts and Fistulas

Inguinal Hernia Treatment

Inguinal hernia in children does not pass by itself and must be repaired surgically.

  • Day surgery (Discharge on the same day)
  • Early intervention against the risk of incarcerated hernia
  • Aesthetic stitching techniques
  • Fast and painless recovery process

Appendicitis Intervention

Early diagnosis is life-saving in appendicitis, which is one of the most important causes of abdominal pain in children.

  • Laparoscopic (closed) method preference
  • Sensitive care in perforated appendicitis
  • Transition to rapid feeding after surgery
  • Difference of an experienced pediatric surgeon

Correct Diagnosis, Expert Surgery

When a surgical decision needs to be made for your child's health problems, we are with you. In Adana, at the clinic of Assoc. Prof. İlknur Banlı Cesur, we adopt a treatment approach that is in line with current medical literature, evidence-based, and maximizes the comfort of our patients. Pediatric general surgery is a vast sea, and we work to offer the safest harbor to our children in this sea.

Umbilical hernias, unlike inguinal hernias, usually tend to close spontaneously. If the hernia is not very large or does not cause a problem, it can be waited until the age of 4. However, inguinal hernias should be operated on as soon as they are diagnosed.

Generally, after routine surgeries such as hernia and appendicitis, children become active within the house within 2-3 days and can return to active play and school life after 1 week. It may only be necessary to wait 4-6 weeks for heavy sports.
