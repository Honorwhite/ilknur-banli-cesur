Children are never "small adults." Their physiological structures, diseases, and surgical needs are entirely unique, making it critical that their operations are performed by a specialist in pediatric surgery.

For parents, the idea of their child needing surgical intervention can be highly distressing. However, developments in modern medical technology, safe pediatric anesthesia techniques, and the experience of specialist physicians guarantee successful outcomes. What is Pediatric Surgery and What Age Group Does It Cover?

Pediatric surgery is the branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis and surgical treatment of conditions from the prenatal period (in the womb) through newborn, infancy, childhood, and adolescence (up to age 18).

Pediatric surgeons use delicate techniques and instruments designed for children's smaller organs and tissues. All thoracic organs except the heart, the digestive tract, urinary tract, reproductive organs, pediatric oncology, and trauma surgery represent major areas of pediatric surgical expertise.

Which Diseases Does Pediatric Surgery Deal With?

Major diseases treated by pediatric surgery specialists include:

  • **Inguinal Region Conditions:** Inguinal hernia, umbilical hernia, hydrocele, and undescended testis surgery.
  • **Newborn Surgery:** Esophageal atresia, intestinal obstructions, diaphragmatic hernia, and anal atresia.
  • **Pediatric Urology:** Circumcision, newborn circumcision, hypospadias (prophet's circumcision), renal pelvic stenosis, and urinary incontinence.
  • **Laparoscopic (Closed) Surgery:** Appendicitis, gallbladder, and inguinal hernia operations in children.
  • **Pediatric Tumor Surgery:** Wilms tumor, neuroblastoma, and other solid tumors.
  • **Other Conditions:** Tongue-tie, umbilical granuloma, pilonidal sinus, skin and soft tissue masses.

Why Choose a Pediatric Surgeon?

Children's fluid balances, blood volumes, response to anesthesia, and tissue healing rates are entirely different from adults. Even a routine procedure like circumcision can affect a child's psychological and physical well-being forever if performed incorrectly.

Pediatric surgeons minimize surgical stress and complication risks thanks to their mastery of child anatomy and child psychology. Therefore, when surgery is required, it must be evaluated by a pediatric surgeon.
